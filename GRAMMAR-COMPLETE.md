## üìñ PARTS OF SPEECH

### 1. NOUNS (Kata Benda)

#### Types of Nouns

##### **Countable Nouns** (Bisa dihitung)
- **Singular**: book, car, employee, manager, office, company
- **Plural**: books, cars, employees, managers, offices, companies

**Rules for Plural**:
- General: +s (book ‚Üí books)
- Ending in -s, -ss, -x, -z, -ch, -sh: +es (bus ‚Üí buses, box ‚Üí boxes)
- Ending in consonant + y: y ‚Üí ies (company ‚Üí companies)
- Ending in vowel + y: +s (boy ‚Üí boys)
- Ending in -f or -fe: ‚Üí ves (leaf ‚Üí leaves, knife ‚Üí knives)
- Irregular: man ‚Üí men, woman ‚Üí women, child ‚Üí children, foot ‚Üí feet

**Usage**:
- ‚úÖ I have **two books**.
- ‚úÖ There are **three employees** in the office.

**Articles with Countable Nouns**:
- ‚úÖ **a** book (singular)
- ‚úÖ **the** books (specific)
- ‚ùå **a** books (wrong - a only with singular)
- ‚úÖ **some** books (plural)

##### **Uncountable Nouns** (Tidak bisa dihitung)
- Abstract: information, advice, knowledge, education, happiness
- Materials: water, air, coffee, sugar, paper, money
- Concepts: time, work, business, traffic, weather

**Usage**:
- ‚úÖ I need **some information**.
- ‚úÖ There is **much traffic**.
- ‚ùå I need **an information**. (wrong - can't use a/an with uncountable)
- ‚ùå **One** water. (wrong - can't count)

**Quantifiers for Uncountable Nouns**:
- Much, a lot of, some, any, a little, a bit of
- ‚ùå many (only for countable)
- ‚úÖ much, a little, a lot of

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **Much** information
- ‚úÖ **A little** money
- ‚úÖ **A lot of** work
- ‚úÖ **Some** water

##### **Collective Nouns** (Koleksi orang/hal)
- team, group, family, company, staff, committee, audience, class

**Usage**:
- **Singular** (unit): The team **is** winning.
- **Plural** (individual members): The team **are** arguing.

**Common Collective Nouns**:
- staff (karyawan)
- management (manajemen)
- committee (komite)
- audience (penonton)
- police (polisi - always plural)

##### **Proper Nouns** (Nama khusus)
- Names: John, Sarah, Google, Microsoft
- Places: Jakarta, Tokyo, America
- Organizations: United Nations, WHO

**Usage**:
- Always starts with CAPITAL letter
- No article needed usually
- ‚úÖ **John** works at **Google**.

##### **Possessive Nouns** (Kepemilikan)
- **Singular**: 's (John's car, the manager's office)
- **Plural**: s' (employees' salaries, companies' profits)
- **Irregular plural**: 's (children's toys, men's room)

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ The **company's** profit
- ‚úÖ The **employees'** benefits
- ‚úÖ **John's** report

#### Noun Functions

##### **Subject** (Subjek)
- The **manager** is busy. (Manager = subject)
- **Meetings** are important.

##### **Object** (Objek)
- Direct Object: I need the **report**.
- Indirect Object: Give **him** the file.

##### **Object of Preposition**
- She works **in the office**.
- The meeting is **on Monday**.

##### **Complement**
- She is a **manager**. (Manager = complement)

### 2. VERBS (Kata Kerja)

#### Types of Verbs

##### **Action Verbs** (Kata kerja aksi)
- work, study, read, write, speak, listen, think, create, develop, manage, communicate

**Examples**:
- She **works** at Google.
- I **manage** the project.

##### **Stative Verbs** (Kata kerja keadaan - TIDAK bisa digunakan dalam continuous tenses)
- Verbs of thinking: know, understand, believe, think (opinion)
- Verbs of feeling: like, love, hate, want, prefer, need
- Verbs of possession: have, own, belong, possess
- Verbs of senses: see, hear, smell, taste, feel
- Verbs of existence: be, exist

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I **know** him. (Correct)
- ‚ùå I am knowing him. (Wrong - stative verb)

##### **Transitive Verbs** (Membutuhkan objek)
- **Require an object**: give, send, show, tell, make, create, develop

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ She **gave** him the report. (with object)
- ‚ùå She gave. (incomplete - needs object)

##### **Intransitive Verbs** (TIDAK membutuhkan objek)
- **No object needed**: arrive, come, go, sleep, sit, stand, lie, die

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ He **arrived** yesterday. (complete - no object needed)
- ‚ùå He arrived the office. (wrong - no preposition)

##### **Phrasal Verbs** (Kata kerja frasa - verb + preposition/particle)
- Common phrasal verbs:
  - **give up** = menyerah
  - **look for** = mencari
  - **run into** = bertemu (tak sengaja)
  - **call off** = membatalkan
  - **fill out** = mengisi (form)
  - **set up** = mendirikan/menyiapkan
  - **turn down** = menolak
  - **put off** = menunda

**Examples**:
- I'm **looking for** a job.
- She **gave up** smoking.
- They **called off** the meeting.

##### **Modal Verbs** (Kata kerja modal - special verbs)
- **List**: can, could, may, might, must, should, would, will, shall
- **Followed by**: base verb (no -s, no -ing, no to)

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I **can help** you.
- ‚ùå I can helps you. (wrong - no -s)
- ‚ùå I can helping you. (wrong - no -ing)
- ‚ùå I can to help you. (wrong - no to)

**Modal Verb Meanings**:
| Modal | Usage | Example |
|-------|-------|---------|
| can | Ability, permission | I can swim. |
| could | Past ability, polite request | Could you help me? |
| may | Possibility, formal permission | It may rain. |
| might | Less certain possibility | It might happen. |
| must | Necessity, obligation | You must finish today. |
| should | Advice, recommendation | You should study. |
| would | Conditional, polite | I would like that. |
| will | Future, prediction | I will go. |
| shall | Formal future, offer | Shall we go? |

##### **Linking Verbs** (Kata kerja penghubung)
- **Link verb to subject complement**: be, seem, appear, look, sound, feel, taste, smell, become, remain

**Examples**:
- She **is** happy.
- He **seems** tired.
- The plan **looks** good.

### 3. ADJECTIVES (Kata Sifat)

#### Types of Adjectives

##### **Descriptive Adjectives**
- Describe quality, size, color, shape, condition, etc.

**Examples**:
- Quality: good, bad, excellent, poor, wonderful
- Size: big, small, large, tiny, huge
- Color: red, blue, green, yellow
- Shape: round, square, oval, triangular
- Condition: old, new, fresh, clean, dirty

**Usage**:
- ‚úÖ A **good** manager.
- ‚úÖ The **large** office.
- ‚úÖ **Red** cars.

##### **Proper Adjectives**
- Derived from proper nouns, start with capital letter

**Examples**:
- **Indonesian** food
- **American** culture
- **Christian** religion
- **European** countries

##### **Compound Adjectives**
- Two or more words acting as single adjective

**Examples**:
- **well-known** company
- **part-time** job
- **full-time** employee
- **high-quality** product
- **three-day** conference
- **five-star** hotel

**Rules**:
- Usually hyphenated
- Never add -s (even with plural nouns)
- ‚úÖ A **three-day** trip. (not three-days)
- ‚úÖ A **five-star** hotel. (not five-stars)

##### **Participial Adjectives**

**Present Participle (-ing)**
- Active meaning - causes the feeling
- boring, interesting, exciting, tiring, confusing

**Examples**:
- The movie was **boring**. (movie caused boredom)
- It's an **interesting** book.

**Past Participle (-ed)**
- Passive meaning - feels the feeling
- bored, interested, excited, tired, confused

**Examples**:
- I was **bored** by the movie. (I felt boredom)
- She is **interested** in art.

**Comparison**:
- **Boring** movie = The movie causes boredom
- **Bored** person = The person feels bored

#### Adjective Position

##### **Before Noun** (Attributive Position)
- ‚úÖ The **good** student
- ‚úÖ **Large** office
- ‚úÖ **Beautiful** day

##### **After Linking Verb** (Predicative Position)
- ‚úÖ The student **is good**.
- ‚úÖ The office **is large**.
- ‚úÖ The day **is beautiful**.

##### **After Noun** (Postpositive Position) - Rare
- Something, someone, anyone, anything
- ‚úÖ Someone **important**
- ‚úÖ Something **interesting**

#### Order of Adjectives
When using multiple adjectives before a noun, follow this order:

**OSASCOMP**:
1. **O**pinion: good, beautiful, excellent
2. **S**ize: big, small, large
3. **A**ge: old, new, young
4. **S**hape: round, square, oval
5. **C**olor: red, blue, green
6. **O**rigin: Indonesian, American, Chinese
7. **M**aterial: wooden, plastic, metal
8. **P**urpose/Noun: tennis (shoes), sleeping (bag)

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ A **beautiful old** house. (Opinion + Age)
- ‚úÖ **Large round** table. (Size + Shape)
- ‚úÖ **Blue plastic** cup. (Color + Material)
- ‚úÖ **Good small Indonesian** restaurant. (Opinion + Size + Origin + Noun)

#### Comparative & Superlative Adjectives

##### **Comparative** (Membandingkan 2 hal)
- **Short adjectives** (1-2 syllables): + -er
  - big ‚Üí **bigger**, fast ‚Üí **faster**, old ‚Üí **older**
- **Long adjectives** (2+ syllables): more + adjective
  - beautiful ‚Üí **more beautiful**, expensive ‚Üí **more expensive**
- **Irregular**:
  - good ‚Üí **better**
  - bad ‚Üí **worse**
  - far ‚Üí **farther/further**

**Usage**:
- ‚úÖ This office is **bigger** than that one.
- ‚úÖ She is **more intelligent** than her brother.

##### **Superlative** (Membandingkan 3+ hal)
- **Short adjectives**: the + -est
  - big ‚Üí **the biggest**, fast ‚Üí **the fastest**
- **Long adjectives**: the most + adjective
  - beautiful ‚Üí **the most beautiful**
- **Irregular**:
  - good ‚Üí **the best**
  - bad ‚Üí **the worst**
  - far ‚Üí **the farthest/furthest**

**Usage**:
- ‚úÖ This is **the biggest** office.
- ‚úÖ She is **the most beautiful** woman here.

### 4. ADVERBS (Kata Keterangan)

#### Types of Adverbs

##### **Adverbs of Time**
- Answer "When?"
- now, then, tomorrow, yesterday, today, soon, later, recently, already, yet, before, after

**Examples**:
- I will do it **tomorrow**.
- She finished **already**.
- Have you eaten **yet**?

##### **Adverbs of Place**
- Answer "Where?"
- here, there, nowhere, everywhere, inside, outside, upstairs, downstairs, away, home, abroad

**Examples**:
- Come **here**.
- She is **outside**.
- Let's go **home**.

##### **Adverbs of Manner**
- Answer "How?"
- Describe how something is done
- Most end in -ly
- quickly, slowly, carefully, badly, well, hard, fast, easily, loudly, quietly

**Examples**:
- She works **quickly**.
- He drives **carefully**.
- She speaks English **well**.

**Important**: Good (adjective) ‚Üí Well (adverb)
- ‚úÖ She is a **good** driver. (adjective)
- ‚úÖ She drives **well**. (adverb)

##### **Adverbs of Frequency**
- Answer "How often?"
- always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, ever, seldom

**Order of Frequency**:
- **Always** (100%)
- **Usually** (80-90%)
- **Often** (60-70%)
- **Sometimes** (50%)
- **Rarely/Seldom** (10-20%)
- **Never** (0%)

**Position**:
- Before main verb: I **always** wake up early.
- After be verb: She is **never** late.
- Between auxiliary & main verb: I have **always** liked it.

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I **always** drink coffee in the morning.
- ‚úÖ She **usually** arrives at 8 AM.
- ‚úÖ He **sometimes** works from home.
- ‚úÖ They **never** miss a meeting.

##### **Adverbs of Degree**
- Answer "How much?"
- very, extremely, really, quite, rather, too, enough, almost, completely, totally

**Examples**:
- It's **very** hot today.
- She is **extremely** intelligent.
- This is **quite** good.
- It's **too** expensive.
- She is **old enough** to vote.

##### **Adverbs of Certainty**
- Answer "How certain?"
- certainly, definitely, probably, possibly, perhaps, maybe, surely

**Examples**:
- She will **definitely** come.
- It will **probably** rain.
- **Maybe** we should leave.

#### Adverb Formation

##### **Regular Formation**
- Most adverbs: adjective + ly
  - quick ‚Üí **quickly**
  - careful ‚Üí **carefully**
  - happy ‚Üí **happily**
  - bad ‚Üí **badly**

##### **Exceptions**:
- good ‚Üí **well** (irregular)
- fast ‚Üí **fast** (same)
- hard ‚Üí **hard** (same)
- early ‚Üí **early** (same)
- late ‚Üí **late** (same)

##### **Spelling Rules**:
- y ‚Üí ily (happy ‚Üí happily)
- le ‚Üí ly (terrible ‚Üí terribly)
- ic ‚Üí ically (fantastic ‚Üí fantastically)
- ll ‚Üí ly (full ‚Üí fully)
- ue ‚Üí ly (true ‚Üí truly)

#### Adverb Position

##### **Initial Position** (Beginning of sentence)
- Emphasizes the adverb
- ‚úÖ **Yesterday**, I went to the office.
- ‚úÖ **Fortunately**, he arrived on time.

##### **Mid Position** (Between subject & verb)
- **Before** main verb
- **After** be verb
- **Between** auxiliary & main verb
- ‚úÖ I **always** wake up early.
- ‚úÖ She is **never** late.
- ‚úÖ I have **already** finished.

##### **Final Position** (End of sentence)
- Most common position
- ‚úÖ I saw her **yesterday**.
- ‚úÖ She speaks English **well**.
- ‚úÖ He works **carefully**.

**Multiple Adverbs** (Order):
1. Manner ‚Üí 2. Place ‚Üí 3. Time
- ‚úÖ She works **diligently** (manner) **in the office** (place) **every day** (time).

### 6. ARTICLES (Artikel: a, an, the)

#### Types of Articles

##### **Indefinite Articles: a, an**
- Used for non-specific or first mention
- Can only be used with singular countable nouns

**"a"**: Before consonant sounds
- ‚úÖ a book, a computer, a manager, a house
- ‚úÖ a university (university starts with "y" sound)
- ‚úÖ a one-way street (one starts with "w" sound)

**"an"**: Before vowel sounds
- ‚úÖ an office, an apple, an hour (h is silent)
- ‚úÖ an MBA (M is pronounced "em")
- ‚úÖ an honest person (h is silent)

**When to use a/an**:
- First mention: I saw **a** bird.
- One of many: She is **a** teacher.
- Job/profession: He is **an** engineer.

##### **Definite Article: the**
- Used for specific or second mention
- Can be used with singular & plural nouns

**When to use "the"**:
- Second mention: I saw **a** bird. **The** bird was blue.
- Specific: **The** manager is in the office. (specific manager)
- Unique items: **the** sun, **the** moon, **the** earth
- Superlatives: **The** best, **the** largest
- Ordinal numbers: **The** first, **the** second
- Specific names of: rivers, oceans, mountain ranges
  - the Nile, the Pacific Ocean, the Rocky Mountains

**When NOT to use "the"**:
- Plural nouns (general): I like books.
- Uncountable nouns (general): Water is essential.
- Proper nouns (people, cities, countries): John, Jakarta, Indonesia
- Meals: breakfast, lunch, dinner

#### Zero Article (No Article)
- Used for:
  - Plural nouns (general): I read books.
  - Uncountable nouns (general): I drink water.
  - Proper nouns: John, Google, Jakarta
  - Meals: Let's have lunch.

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I like **(0)** music.
- ‚úÖ **(0)** Water is essential.
- ‚úÖ **(0)** John works at Google.

#### Article Summary

| Noun Type | First Mention | Second Mention | Specific |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------|
| Singular Countable | a/an | the | the |
| Plural Countable | (0) / some | the | the |
| Uncountable | (0) / some | the | the |

**Examples**:
- I bought **a** book. (first mention)
- **The** book was interesting. (second mention)
- I like books. (plural, general)
- **The** books on the shelf are old. (specific plural)
- I drink water. (uncountable, general)
- **The** water in the glass is cold. (specific)

### 7. PRONOUNS (Kata Ganti)

#### Types of Pronouns

##### **Subject Pronouns** (Pronoun Subjek)
- Used as subject of sentence

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | I | we |
| 2nd | you | you |
| 3rd | he, she, it | they |

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **I** work here.
- ‚úÖ **She** is a manager.
- ‚úÖ **They** are employees.

##### **Object Pronouns** (Pronoun Objek)
- Used as object of verb or preposition

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | me | us |
| 2nd | you | you |
| 3rd | him, her, it | them |

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I saw **him** yesterday. (object of verb)
- ‚úÖ Talk to **her**. (object of preposition)
- ‚úÖ Give **them** the report. (object of verb)

**Subject vs Object**:
- ‚úÖ **He** helped me. (He = subject, me = object)
- ‚úÖ I helped **him**. (I = subject, him = object)

##### **Possessive Adjectives** (Kepunyaan sebelum noun)
- Used before noun

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | my | our |
| 2nd | your | your |
| 3rd | his, her, its | their |

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **My** book is here.
- ‚úÖ **Her** office is big.
- ‚úÖ **Their** manager is busy.

##### **Possessive Pronouns** (Kepunyaan - pengganti noun)
- Used alone (replace noun)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | mine | ours |
| 2nd | yours | yours |
| 3rd | his, hers, its | theirs |

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ This book is **mine**.
- ‚úÖ That office is **hers**.
- ‚úÖ Those reports are **theirs**.

**Possessive Adjective vs Possessive Pronoun**:
- ‚úÖ **My** car. (adjective - before noun)
- ‚úÖ The car is **mine**. (pronoun - alone)

##### **Reflexive Pronouns** (Pronoun Refleksif)
- Used when subject = object

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | myself | ourselves |
| 2nd | yourself | yourselves |
| 3rd | himself, herself, itself | themselves |

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I hurt **myself**.
- ‚úÖ She looked at **herself** in the mirror.
- ‚úÖ They helped **themselves** to the food.

**When to use**:
- When subject and object are the same person
- ‚úÖ I taught **myself** to play guitar. (I = subject, myself = object)
- ‚úÖ She looked at **herself**. (She = subject, herself = object)

##### **Demonstrative Pronouns** (Pronoun Penunjuk)
- Used to point at specific things

| Near | Far |
|------|-----|
| Singular | this, that |
| Plural | these, those |

**As Pronouns**:
- ‚úÖ **This** is my book.
- ‚úÖ **That** is your office.
- ‚úÖ **These** are my friends.
- ‚úÖ **Those** are their cars.

**As Adjectives** (before nouns):
- ‚úÖ **This** book is mine.
- ‚úÖ **That** office is yours.
- ‚úÖ **These** books are new.
- ‚úÖ **Those** offices are old.

##### **Indefinite Pronouns** (Pronoun Tak Tentu)
- Refer to non-specific people or things

**Singular Indefinite Pronouns** (always singular):
- someone, somebody, everyone, everybody, no one, nobody
- anyone, anybody
- something, everything, nothing, anything
- each, either, neither, one, another, the other
- none, nobody, no one

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **Everyone** is here. (singular verb)
- ‚úÖ **Somebody** is at the door.
- ‚úÖ **Nothing** is impossible.
- ‚úÖ **Each** of them **has** a unique skill. (singular verb)

**Plural Indefinite Pronouns** (always plural):
- both, few, several, many

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **Both** are correct. (plural verb)
- ‚úÖ **Few** have arrived.
- ‚úÖ **Several** are missing.

**Can be Singular or Plural**:
- all, some, any, none, most
- Depends on the noun they refer to

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **All** of the money **is** gone. (money = uncountable = singular)
- ‚úÖ **All** of the students **are** here. (students = plural = plural)
- ‚úÖ **Some** of the cake **is** left. (cake = singular)
- ‚úÖ **Some** of the students **are** absent. (students = plural)

##### **Interrogative Pronouns** (Pronoun Tanya)
- Used to ask questions
- who, whom, whose, which, what

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **Who** is there?
- ‚úÖ **Whom** did you see?
- ‚úÖ **Whose** book is this?
- ‚úÖ **Which** one do you want?
- ‚úÖ **What** is happening?

**Who vs Whom**:
- **Who**: Subject of question
  - ‚úÖ **Who** called you?
- **Whom**: Object of question
  - ‚úÖ **Whom** did you call?

##### **Relative Pronouns** (Pronoun Relatif)
- Connect clauses

| Relative Pronoun | Usage | Example |
|------------------|-------|---------|
| **who** | People (subject) | The man **who** called |
| **whom** | People (object) | The man **whom** I met |
| **which** | Things, animals | The book **which** I read |
| **that** | People or things (restrictive) | The car **that** I bought |
| **whose** | Possession | The student **whose** name is... |
| **where** | Places | The office **where** I work |
| **when** | Times | The day **when** I arrived |

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ The man **who** called me is my friend.
- ‚úÖ The book **which** I read is interesting.
- ‚úÖ The woman **whose** car was stolen called the police.

### 8. CONJUNCTIONS (Kata Hubung)

#### Coordinating Conjunctions (Fanboys)
Connect words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance

**FANBOYS**:
- **F**or
- **A**nd
- **N**or
- **B**ut
- **O**r
- **Y**et
- **S**o

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I like coffee **and** tea.
- ‚úÖ I can't go, **but** I'll try later.
- ‚úÖ Should I call **or** email?
- ‚úÖ She didn't study, **so** she failed.

**Rules**:
- When connecting two complete sentences, use comma + conjunction
- ‚úÖ I like coffee, **and** she likes tea.

#### Subordinating Conjunctions
Connect dependent clause to independent clause

**Common Subordinating Conjunctions**:
- **Time**: after, as, as soon as, before, by the time, now that, once, since, till, until, when, whenever, while
- **Place**: where, wherever
- **Manner**: as, as if, as though
- **Cause/Reason**: because, since, now that, as
- **Purpose**: in order that, so that, in case, lest
- **Condition**: if, unless, provided that, in case, even if, as long as
- **Concession**: although, even though, though, whereas, while
- **Result**: so...that, such...that
- **Comparison**: as...as, more...than, less...than

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ I'll call you **when** I arrive.
- ‚úÖ She stayed home **because** she was sick.
- ‚úÖ **If** it rains, I'll stay inside.
- ‚úÖ **Although** it's expensive, I'll buy it.

**Punctuation Rules**:
- Dependent clause first: **,** + independent clause
  - ‚úÖ **When** I arrived, **he was sleeping**.
- Independent clause first: no comma
  - ‚úÖ **He was sleeping when I arrived**.

#### Correlative Conjunctions
Work in pairs

**Pairs**:
- both...and
- either...or
- neither...nor
- not only...but also
- whether...or
- not...but

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ **Both** John **and** Mary are coming.
- ‚úÖ You can have **either** coffee **or** tea.
- ‚úÖ **Neither** John **nor** Mary came.
- ‚úÖ **Not only** is she smart, **but also** hardworking.

**Subject-Verb Agreement with Correlative Conjunctions**:
- Both A and B: Plural verb
  - ‚úÖ **Both** John **and** Mary **are** here.
- Neither A nor B: Verb agrees with nearest noun
  - ‚úÖ **Neither** John **nor** the girls **are** here. (nearest = girls = plural)
  - ‚úÖ **Neither** the girls **nor** John **is** here. (nearest = John = singular)
- Either A or B: Verb agrees with nearest noun
  - ‚úÖ **Either** John **or** Mary **is** coming.
  - ‚úÖ **Either** the teachers **or** the principal **is** coming.
- Not only A but also B: Verb agrees with B (nearest noun)
  - ‚úÖ **Not only** the students **but also** the teacher **is** happy.

## üìê SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT

### Basic Rules

#### Rule 1: Singular Subject ‚Üí Singular Verb
- ‚úÖ The manager **is** busy.
- ‚úÖ She **works** hard.
- ‚úÖ It **rains** often.

#### Rule 2: Plural Subject ‚Üí Plural Verb
- ‚úÖ The managers **are** busy.
- ‚úÖ They **work** hard.
- ‚úÖ They **come** early.

#### Rule 3: Singular Verbs (with s/es)
- ‚úÖ He **work**s**. (singular verb = with -s)
- ‚úÖ She **eat**s**. (singular verb = with -s)
- ‚úÖ It **run**s**. (singular verb = with -s)

#### Rule 4: Plural Verbs (without s/es)
- ‚úÖ They **work**. (plural verb = no -s)
- ‚úÖ We **eat**. (plural verb = no -s)
- ‚úÖ You **come**. (plural verb = no -s)

### Tricky Subjects

#### Each, Every, Either, Neither
- Always **SINGULAR**
- ‚úÖ Each employee **has** a desk.
- ‚úÖ Every student **is** present.
- ‚úÖ Either option **works**.
- ‚úÖ Neither car **is** available.

#### Indefinite Pronouns (Singular)
- someone, somebody, everyone, everybody, no one, nobody
- anyone, anybody
- something, everything, nothing, anything
- each, either, neither, one, another

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Everyone **is** here.
- ‚úÖ Somebody **is** knocking.
- ‚úÖ Nothing **is** impossible.
- ‚úÖ Each of them **has** a skill.

#### Compound Subjects (and/or)

**"And" = Plural** (usually)
- ‚úÖ John and Mary **are** here.
- ‚úÖ The manager and the assistant **are** working.
- ‚úÖ Bread and butter **are** delicious.

**Exception**: Singular if refers to single entity
- ‚úÖ Bread and butter **is** a common breakfast. (single concept)

**"Or" / "Nor" = Nearest noun**
- ‚úÖ John or Mary **is** coming. (nearest = Mary = singular)
- ‚úÖ John or his friends **are** coming. (nearest = friends = plural)
- ‚úÖ Neither John nor Mary **is** coming. (nearest = Mary = singular)
- ‚úÖ Neither the students nor the teacher **is** here. (nearest = teacher = singular)

#### Collective Nouns
- team, group, family, company, committee, staff, class

**Singular** (as a unit):
- ‚úÖ The team **is** winning. (unit)
- ‚úÖ The committee **has** decided. (as a group)

**Plural** (as individuals):
- ‚úÖ The team **are** arguing. (individuals)
- ‚úÖ The class **are** taking notes. (individuals)

#### Quantifiers

**A number of = Plural**
- ‚úÖ **A number of** people **are** coming.

**The number of = Singular**
- ‚úÖ **The number of** people **is** increasing.

**All / Some / Most / Any / None**
- Depends on the noun (countable vs uncountable, singular vs plural)

**Countable, Plural ‚Üí Plural verb**
- ‚úÖ All of the students **are** here.
- ‚úÖ Some of the books **are** missing.

**Uncountable ‚Üí Singular verb**
- ‚úÖ All of the water **is** gone.
- ‚úÖ Some of the money **is** missing.

**Uncountable, Plural ‚Üí Plural verb**
- ‚úÖ All of the students **are** here.

#### Titles and Names (Always Singular)
- Even if ends in s, still singular
- ‚úÖ "The United States" **is** a country.
- ‚úÖ "Mathematics" **is** my favorite subject.
- ‚úÖ "The New York Times" **is** a newspaper.

#### Time, Distance, Money (Singular)
- Treated as a single unit
- ‚úÖ Ten dollars **is** too much. (single amount)
- ‚úÖ Three hours **is** a long time. (single duration)
- ‚úÖ Two miles **is** a long distance.

### Special Cases

#### Inverted Sentences
- Subject comes after verb

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Here **comes** the bus. (singular subject = bus = singular verb)
- ‚úÖ There **are** three people waiting. (plural subject = people = plural verb)
- ‚úÖ Never **have** I seen such beauty. (I = singular)

#### Intervening Phrases
- Ignore phrases between subject and verb

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ The box of chocolates **is** on the table. (ignore "of chocolates", subject = box = singular)
- ‚úÖ The manager, along with his team, **is** here. (ignore "along with his team", subject = manager = singular)
- ‚úÖ The list of items **includes** 10 things. (subject = list = singular)

#### Pronoun Agreement
- Pronoun must agree with its antecedent in number & gender

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Each student must bring **his or her** book. (singular ‚Üí singular)
- ‚úÖ Everyone should bring **their** book. (informal - singular subject, plural pronoun now accepted)
- ‚úÖ The team won **its** game. (singular collective noun)

## üìù PRACTICE TIPS

### How to Master Grammar

1. **Learn the Rules**
   - Understand the basic rules first
   - Move to advanced rules
   - Practice with examples

2. **Identify the Context**
   - Look for time markers
   - Understand the meaning
   - Apply the correct rule

3. **Practice with TOEIC Questions**
   - Focus on what's being tested
   - Eliminate wrong answers
   - Choose the best option

4. **Learn Common Mistakes**
   - Identify patterns of errors
   - Practice to avoid them
   - Review regularly

5. **Use Context Clues**
   - Words around the blank
   - Logical flow
   - Consistency
